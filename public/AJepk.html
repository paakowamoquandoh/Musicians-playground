AJ NELSON

Biography

Adjei Nelson Otumfour, better known by his stage name AJ Nelson is a Ghanaian recording artist and activist. He is most famous for the singles "Same Girl”,"Faith” and “Power to the People” and is the first artist to rap with the Bono language in his songs.


In 2015, AJ Nelson led a campaign titled “Power To the People'' to encourage the youth in Ghana and other parts of Africa to speak against the increasing corruption and conflict in Africa. According to AJ Nelson, the people most affected need to be empowered to voice out their leaders. The campaign got the attention of the renowned Communication Director at the World Bank, Mr. Kofi Tsikata, and media personality Berla Mundi who all joined the campaign.


In the same period, AJ Nelson released a hip-hop song titled “Power to the People''. In November 2015, the song was ranked the 3rd most downloaded Ghanaian Hip Hop Song on iTunes. AJ Nelson was ranked the 3rd top-selling artist on iTunes in November 2015

Career Highlights

  • Born in Accra, Ghana, AJ Nelson (real name Adjei Yaw Nelson Otumfour) is a hip-hop artist who grew up in Dormaa Ahenkro. His music is inspired by the environment in which he lives.

  • He joined the music scene when U.S'. president Barack Obama visited Ghana in 2009. His music is a fusion of bono dialect, the Asante Twi, Pidgin and English languages. He has featured in a historic collaboration that was used to welcome Barack Obama.

  • He proceeded to release a debut single called 'Same Girl' (September 2010) which featured Quabena Maphia. Shortly afterwards, he released his second single titled 'Faith' featuring Jay Ghartey.

  • On 10 October 2015, he released a 16-track debut album titled 'Soul Food'. The album was released on online stores such as iTunes, YouTube, Music Key, Apple Music, Spotify, Amazon, Google Play, Deezer, Spinlet and Tidal.

  • In 2015, he teamed up with VGMA Best Song Writer of the Year and Best Female Vocalist award winner Wiyaala on a hip-hop song titled 'Power To The People', a song that seeks to empower the youth. Shortly after its release, the song was on iTunes' Top Selling artistes charts.

  • UK’s popular entertainment website called We Plug Good Music rated the song as the Best New Music of the Week alongside songs such as Coldplay’s 'Adventure Of A Lifetime', Mr. Drastick’s 'They Wanna Know', Emma McGrath’s 'Sit With Me', Chris Brown’s 'Fine By Me', Illmaculate + OnlyOne’s 'Only & Ill', Booda French’s 'The Feeling', and Weltuntergäng + Arm & Hässlich’s 'Untitled'.

  • AJ Nelson released his debut album Africa Rise in November 2018. The album comprised of 16 tracks all recorded. The album as described, is the best album to be recorded by a rapper in Ghana" The album featured top Ghanaian and internationally recognised artist including Worlasi, Cabum, Ayat, Cheche, and Ohene Savant. The album walks musc loves through he journey of modern slavery , colonisation, the corrupt system created by our leaders and harship in Africa as a result of greed and recklessness. Cheche produced, mixed and mastered 50 percent of the album while Genius Selection, the ReapRZ, Azee Burner, Shizzy and Ohene Savant did the rest of the production job. In 2019, AJ Nelson ended the year with an Africa Rise album concert. Acoording to AJ Nelson "This album has earned me a lot respect among legends and the people who are musically inclined and always on the lookout to an artist as raw as myself in term of my content." " Africa Rise album has inspired me to put together my own band. I believe I'm the first rapper based in Ghana to do so.". The concert saw live performances from artists including Okyeame Kwame, Lady Jay, Suzz Blaq, and 6six Strings.
